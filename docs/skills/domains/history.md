# History — domain overlay

Base: `SIMPLE-JSON-LLM-CONTEXT.md`. Craft: the `lean_on` workflows.

## Mindset
History is **place and time.** Anchor events in space (a map) and sequence (a timeline), then let
regions, borders, and routes change so the viewer watches history move. Orient before you narrate.

## Defaults
- Theme `parchment`. Compositions `map`, `timeline`, `comparison`.
- Palette: `map`, `timeline`, `legend`, `stat`, `text`. No equations or data charts.

## Lean on these workflows
- **[mapping]** — the core tool: orient with regions/cities/legend, target places by name, grow borders
  over time, routes as temporary flows.
- **[timeline]** — era bands + events + a sweeping playhead, synced to the map's year.
- **[comparison]** — peak vs collapse, two rival powers.
- **[labeling]** — a running year or a legacy figure (area, population) for scale.

## Named approaches (lenses)
- `map-over-time` — orient, grow regions with conquest/migration flows, place events on a timeline.
- `timeline-narrative` — chronology leads: era bands, events, tour.
- `compare-states` — two empires or two moments side by side.

## Guardrails
No decorative effects. Temporary marks (arrows) cleaned up. Map state, playhead year, and labels must
agree. **Do not invent facts, dates, or borders** — attribute only to supplied sources.

## A good lesson
"The Mongol Empire" — orient Eurasia, grow borders 1206→1260 with conquest flows and a rising year, an
era timeline synced to the map, then peak vs the four khanates with a legacy area stat.
