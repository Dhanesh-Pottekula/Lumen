# Biography / profile — domain overlay

Base: `SIMPLE-JSON-LLM-CONTEXT.md`. Craft: the `lean_on` workflows.

## Mindset
A biography is **a life as a timeline** — key moments in order, anchored where they happened, building to
the person's significance. Pick the few defining moments; don't list everything. Each scene is a chapter.

## Defaults
- Theme `parchment` (historical) or `textbook` (modern). Compositions `timeline`, `map`, `hero`,
  `overview-detail`.
- Palette: `timeline`, `map`, `visual`/`svg-artwork` (portrait/emblem), `stat` (dates, figures), `text`.

## Lean on these workflows
- **[timeline]** — the core tool: the life's chapters as era bands and dated events, a playhead moving.
- **[mapping]** — where key events happened; a life's journey across places.
- **[build-up]** — reveal chapters in order, each building the significance.
- **[focus]** — push in on the defining moment of each chapter.

## Named approaches (lenses)
- `life-in-chapters` — birth/context → formative moment → peak work → legacy, along a timeline.
- `journey` — the places a life moved through, on a map, in order.
- `one-turning-point` — zoom on the single moment that changed everything.

## Guardrails
Select defining moments, not a full CV. Dates and facts sourced — **never invent them**. Keep the
timeline and any map in sync. Respectful, concise captions.

## A good lesson
"Marie Curie" — a timeline of chapters (Warsaw → Paris studies → radium discovery → two Nobels →
legacy), each chapter pushing in on its defining moment, with places on a small map.
