# Earth science & geography — domain overlay

Base: `SIMPLE-JSON-LLM-CONTEXT.md`. Craft: the `lean_on` workflows.

## Mindset
This is **place and process on Earth.** Anchor everything on a map, then show a system *flow* or a
process *change* it over time — water cycles, plates move, climate shifts, populations spread.

## Defaults
- Theme `parchment` (maps) or `textbook` (systems). Compositions `map`, `process`, `split`, `timeline`.
- Palette: `map`, `svg-artwork` (a system diagram), `effect` (flow), `legend`, `stat`.

## Lean on these workflows
- **[mapping]** — the core tool: orient, then change regions/values over space and time.
- **[motion]/[effects]** — directed flow for cycles (water, wind, currents, migration).
- **[timeline]** — for change over geological or historical time.
- **[comparison]** — two regions, or before/after a process.

## Named approaches (lenses)
- `map-a-system` — orient, then trace the cycle/flow across the map.
- `change-over-time` — a value or boundary shifting on the map with the years.
- `compare-places` — two regions/climates side by side.

## Guardrails
Keep the projection stable. Flows temporary and directional. Data honest and sourced. No decorative
effects — flow must represent a real earth process.

## A good lesson
"The water cycle" — a map/landscape with directed `effect` flows: evaporation up, transport across,
precipitation down, runoff back to sea, labelled at each stage.
