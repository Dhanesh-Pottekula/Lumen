# Story / narrative — domain overlay

Base: `SIMPLE-JSON-LLM-CONTEXT.md`. Craft: the `lean_on` workflows. A "domain" isn't only a school
subject — a *story* is a domain too, with its own craft.

## Mindset
A narrative is an **arc**: setup → rising tension → turn → resolution. The screen should carry the
emotional beat, not just the facts. Use pacing, camera, and one clear focal subject per moment to make
the story *move*. Show the event; let a caption carry only what the picture can't.

## Defaults
- Theme by mood: `parchment` (myth/history), `chalkboard` (somber), `textbook` (clean/modern).
- Compositions `hero` (a moment), `split` (two characters/forces), `overview-detail`.
- Palette: `svg-artwork`/`visual` (characters, sets, props), `motion` (a journey), `text` (narration).

## Lean on these workflows
- **[build-up]** — the core tool: pace the arc in beats; each beat is a story sentence; land the turn.
- **[focus]** — camera and emphasis carry emotion: push in on a decision, pull out at a reveal.
- **[motion]** — a character's journey (`along` a path), an object's fate (a `fall`).
- **[timeline]** — when the story spans time (a life, a saga).
- **[labeling]** — sparse narration captions; the visual leads.

## Named approaches (lenses)
- `arc` — setup → rising action → climax → resolution, one beat-group each.
- `journey` — a path across a scene/map with events along the way.
- `two-forces` — protagonist vs antagonist in a `split`, converging to the clash.

## Guardrails
One focal subject per moment. Captions short — the picture tells it. Effects/camera serve the emotion,
never decorate. Keep the world consistent scene to scene. Don't contradict a sourced retelling.

## A good lesson
"Icarus" — setup (father and son build wings), rising action (Icarus climbs, `motion` up, camera pushing
higher), the turn (wings melt near the sun), resolution (the `fall` to the sea), one somber caption each.
