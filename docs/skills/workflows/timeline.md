# Workflow: Timeline — the story in time

A timeline answers "when" and "in what order." Use it whenever sequence or duration is the point.

## Craft

- **Bands for eras, marks for events.** Give the period colored era bands (phases, reigns, stages) and
  place the key events as marks. The bands give shape; the events give specifics.
- **Sweep a playhead.** Move a playhead across the axis so time visibly advances — a year climbing, a
  phase entered. A static timeline is a diagram; a moving one is a story.
- **`tour` the events in order.** Walk the events one at a time (camera/attention) so the viewer builds
  the sequence, rather than seeing everything at once.
- **Keep it synced.** If the timeline sits beside a map (history) or a process (biology), the playhead's
  position and the other view's state must agree at every moment.

## Reach for it when

Chronology leads the lesson (a reign, the run-up to an event, a life, a product roadmap, the stages of
a process). If "what happened first / next / last" is the question, this is the spine.

## Pitfalls

- Dumping all events at once → `tour` them.
- Playhead and companion view disagree on the year/state → sync them.
- Using a timeline for something that isn't ordered in time → use `comparison` or a chart instead.

Domains that lean on this: **history**, **biography**, **news**, **how-to/process**, **product**.
