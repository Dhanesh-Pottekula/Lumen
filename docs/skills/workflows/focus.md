# Workflow: Focus — directing the eye

At any moment there should be one clear thing to look at. Focus is the craft of making sure the viewer
looks *there* — and never wondering where to look.

## The tools, and when each fits

- **Camera** (`shot`: overview → detail; `move`: cut/move/push) — change *what's in frame*. Push in on
  the moment that matters; pull out to reveal scale or context. `push` for drama, `move` for a pan,
  `cut` for an abrupt change.
- **`attention`** — a temporary mark that says "here": `callout` (label with a leader), `pointer`
  (arrow), `highlight`/`box`/`brackets`/`encircle` (frame a region), `spotlight`/`dim` (isolate by
  darkening the rest), `converge`/`rings` (draw the eye inward).
- **`emphasize`** — a quick self-motion on an object (`punch`/`pulse`/`shake`/`wiggle`) to say "this
  one, now" without adding a mark.
- **`label`** — attach a name to a part, positioned so it doesn't cover the subject.

## Craft

- **One focus at a time.** If two things pulse at once, neither is emphasized. Sequence attention across
  beats.
- **Attention is temporary.** A callout, pointer, or highlight is a teaching mark — remove it when its
  job is done (many auto-clear; don't leave a stack of arrows on screen).
- **Point at the thing, on the thing.** Attach callouts/labels to the exact part anchor
  (`neuron.soma`, `plot.peak`, `map.beijing`), positioned beside the subject, never on top of it.
- **Camera encodes meaning too.** Zoom in to say "this detail matters"; zoom out to say "here's the
  whole." Don't move the camera without a reason — motion for its own sake is disorienting.

## Pitfalls

- Everything highlighted → nothing is; focus one thing per beat.
- Callout covers the subject → offset it (`relative` placement).
- Gratuitous camera moves → only move to reveal or to emphasize.
- Attention marks pile up → clean them.

Domains that lean on this: **all** — every scene needs a clear focal point.
