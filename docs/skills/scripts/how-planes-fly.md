# Visual Script — "How Airplanes Fly"

**Runtime:** ~1:45. **Background:** clean sky-blueprint. **Recurring anchor:** a **wing cross-section
(airfoil)** we keep returning to and adding to. **Voice:** a curious teacher turning an everyday
mystery into simple physics — every idea shown, not told.

Objects list **IN** (appear · where · how) · behavior · **OUT** (removed · how).

## SCENE 1 — 400 Tonnes in the Air (0:00 – 0:10)
**Goal:** the hook — this shouldn't be possible.
**Sees:** A jet glides across a blue sky. A weight tag reads "400 tonnes." How does it not fall?
- **Plane** — glides left→right, center. IN 0:00 (fade). STAYS to 0:10.
- **Title** "How does 400 tonnes stay up?" — center. IN 0:04 (word-by-word). OUT 0:10.
**VO:** "A loaded jumbo jet weighs four hundred tonnes — as much as a small building. So how on earth
does it stay in the sky?"

## SCENE 2 — Four Forces (0:10 – 0:24)
**Goal:** the whole of flight is a tug-of-war of four forces.
**Sees:** The plane freezes and four arrows grow from it: **lift up**, **weight down**, **thrust
forward**, **drag back**.
- **Plane + 4 force arrows** — center. IN 0:12 (arrows grow). STAYS to 0:24.
- **"Lift beats weight = you fly" callout** on the lift arrow. IN 0:18. OUT 0:24.
**VO:** "Every plane lives in a balance of four forces. Weight pulls it down; lift holds it up. Thrust
drives it forward; drag holds it back. Fly, and lift is winning. So where does lift come from?"

## SCENE 3 — The Secret Is the Wing (0:24 – 0:36)
**Goal:** introduce the airfoil — curved on top, flatter below.
**Sees:** Zoom to a **wing cross-section** — curved and longer over the top, flatter underneath.
- **Airfoil** — center. IN 0:26 (draw). STAYS (the anchor).
- **"Curved top, flat bottom" caption** — footer. IN 0:30. OUT 0:36.
**VO:** "The answer is the shape of the wing — the airfoil. Curved and long over the top, flatter
underneath. That small difference is everything."

## SCENE 4 — Air Races Over the Top (0:36 – 0:50)
**Goal:** air splits and moves faster over the curved top.
**Sees:** Streamlines flow at the wing, split, and the **top stream races ahead** of the bottom.
- **Airfoil + two streamlines** — IN 0:38 (flow on). STAYS to 0:50.
- **"Faster over the top" callout** on the top streamline. IN 0:44. OUT 0:50.
**VO:** "As the wing cuts through the air, the flow splits. Over the longer, curved top it has to move
faster than the air slipping underneath."

## SCENE 5 — Faster Air, Lower Pressure (0:50 – 1:04)
**Goal:** the pressure difference — the actual lift.
**Sees:** Small down-pressure marks appear on top (**low pressure**), bigger up-marks below (**high
pressure**). The net is a firm **push upward** = lift.
- **Airfoil + low-pressure (top) + high-pressure (bottom) arrows** — IN 0:52 (build). STAYS to 1:04.
- **"Net push up = LIFT" callout** — IN 0:58. OUT 1:04.
**VO:** "Faster air has lower pressure. So the top of the wing is sucked up while the higher pressure
below pushes up. Add it up, and the wing is shoved upward — that's lift."

## SCENE 6 — Tilt It: Push Air Down (1:04 – 1:18)
**Goal:** angle of attack — Newton's third law.
**Sees:** The wing **tilts** into the wind; the airflow leaving it is **deflected downward**. Air pushed
down → wing pushed up.
- **Tilted airfoil + downwash arrow** — IN 1:06 (tilt + deflect). STAYS to 1:18.
- **"Push air down → wing pushed up" callout** — IN 1:12. OUT 1:18.
**VO:** "There's a second half. Tilt the wing, and it flings air downward. Push air down, and by
Newton's third law the air pushes the wing up. Shape and angle together make the lift."

## SCENE 7 — More Speed, Much More Lift (1:18 – 1:30)
**Goal:** lift grows with the square of speed.
**Sees:** A curve of **lift vs speed** climbs steeply (speed squared) — double the speed, four times the
lift.
- **Lift-vs-speed curve** — IN 1:20 (draw). STAYS to 1:30.
- **"Double speed → 4× lift" callout** — IN 1:26. OUT 1:30.
**VO:** "Lift grows with the square of speed. Go twice as fast and you get four times the lift — which is
why planes must race down a runway before they can leave the ground."

## SCENE 8 — Too Much Angle: Stall (1:30 – 1:42)
**Goal:** tilt too far and lift collapses.
**Sees:** A curve of **lift vs angle** rises… then suddenly **drops off a cliff** — the stall.
- **Lift-vs-angle curve** — IN 1:32 (draw, then the drop). STAYS to 1:42.
- **"Too steep → stall!" callout** at the peak/drop. IN 1:38. OUT 1:42.
**VO:** "But tilt too far and the smooth flow breaks away. Lift doesn't just fade — it collapses. That's
a stall, and every pilot learns to fear it."

## SCENE 9 — Recap (1:42 – 1:52)
**Goal:** flight in one line.
**Sees:** The airfoil returns with a small up-arrow; a takeaway settles.
- **Airfoil + lift arrow** — center. IN 1:42. STAYS to end.
- **Takeaway** "A wing turns speed into lift — and that is flight." — IN 1:46 (word-by-word). STAYS.
**VO:** "So there's no magic. A cleverly shaped wing, moving fast enough, turns speed into lift — and
lift into flight."
