# Visual Script — "Climate Change: How We're Warming the Planet"

**Runtime:** ~2:00. **Background:** deep space-to-sky gradient in early scenes, shifting to a warm
paper/observatory tone for data. **Recurring anchor:** planet Earth — it returns in most scenes as the
subject we keep coming back to. **Voice:** calm, evidence-first teacher; never preachy, always *showing*.

Every scene proves one claim with a picture. Objects list **IN** (appear · where · how), behavior, and
**OUT** (removed · how). Nothing lingers unexplained.

---

## SCENE 1 — The Thin Blue Line (0:00 – 0:11)
**Goal:** the thing we're changing is a paper-thin shell.
**Sees:** Earth hangs against black. The camera drifts to the horizon's edge, where a **razor-thin blue
band** of atmosphere glows. It looks impossibly fragile against the size of the planet.
- **Earth** — full sphere, center-right. IN 0:00 (fades from black). STAYS to 0:11.
- **Atmosphere band** — a thin luminous blue arc hugging the limb. IN 0:03 (glows on). STAYS to 0:11.
- **Title** "The layer that keeps us alive" — lower third. IN 0:05 (word-by-word). OUT 0:11.
- **Camera** — slow push toward the horizon edge 0:02–0:09.
**VO:** "This thin blue shell is all that separates us from the cold of space. It keeps Earth warm — and
we are quietly changing what it's made of."

---

## SCENE 2 — The Natural Greenhouse (0:11 – 0:32)
**Goal:** show the mechanism — sunlight in, heat out, some heat bounced back by gases.
**Sees:** We cut to a side-on cross-section: the Sun at left, Earth's ground as a curved surface at the
bottom, the atmosphere as a soft band above it. **Yellow rays** stream down from the Sun and strike the
ground. The ground glows and re-emits **red wavy arrows (infrared heat)** rising upward. Floating **CO₂
and H₂O molecules** in the band intercept some red arrows and **bounce them back down** — the ground
stays warm. A thermometer climbs to a comfortable mark.
- **Sun** — glowing disk, upper left. IN 0:11. STAYS to 0:32.
- **Ground surface** — curved band, bottom. IN 0:11. STAYS.
- **Atmosphere band + molecules** — a translucent band holding a few CO₂/H₂O molecule icons. IN 0:13.
  STAYS.
- **Incoming sunlight** — 4 straight yellow rays, Sun→ground. IN 0:14 (stream on). STAYS.
- **Outgoing infrared** — red wavy arrows rising from the ground. IN 0:18 (rise). STAYS.
- **Bounced-back arrows** — some red arrows hit a molecule and redirect back down (a little zig). IN 0:22.
  STAYS.
- **Thermometer** — right side, rises to "+15°C · comfortable". IN 0:26 (fills). STAYS.
- **Callout** on a molecule: "Greenhouse gases trap heat." IN 0:24. OUT 0:32.
**VO:** "Sunlight pours in and warms the ground. The ground radiates that heat back out as infrared. But
greenhouse gases — carbon dioxide, water vapour — catch some of it and send it back down. That trapped
warmth is why Earth is a livable fifteen degrees, not a frozen rock."

---

## SCENE 3 — Without the Blanket (0:32 – 0:42)
**Goal:** contrast — the greenhouse effect is *good and necessary*; the problem is *changing its
strength*.
**Sees:** Split screen. LEFT: the same scene, molecules present, thermometer at **+15°C**, a green
"livable" tint. RIGHT: molecules removed, most red arrows escape to space, thermometer plummets to
**−18°C**, a blue frozen tint with frost creeping in.
- **Left panel (with gases)** — IN 0:32 (fade). OUT 0:42.
- **Right panel (no gases, escaping heat, frost)** — IN 0:35 (fade). OUT 0:42.
- **Two thermometers** — +15°C (left) / −18°C (right). IN with their panels. OUT 0:42.
- **Divider label** "The greenhouse effect is natural — and necessary." — center. IN 0:38. OUT 0:42.
**VO:** "Don't mistake the greenhouse effect for the villain. Without it, Earth would be a frozen
minus-eighteen. The danger isn't that it exists — it's that we're making it stronger."

---

## SCENE 4 — What We're Adding (0:42 – 1:00)
**Goal:** humans burn fossil fuels → extra CO₂ pours into the air.
**Sees:** A ground-level city scene: a **factory** and a **car**. From their stacks/exhaust, small
**CO₂ molecules stream upward** and join a thickening haze in the sky band — the band visibly darkens as
more molecules gather. Then the view swaps to a graph: the **Keeling curve** — atmospheric CO₂ climbing
year over year in a jagged, relentless upward line, from ~280 to 420 ppm.
- **Factory + car** — bottom, small. IN 0:42 (fade). OUT 0:54 (fade as we cut to graph).
- **CO₂ stream** — molecules rising from stacks into the band. IN 0:44 (emit upward). OUT 0:54.
- **Thickening haze band** — sky band darkens as molecules collect. IN 0:46. OUT 0:54.
- **Keeling curve chart** — CO₂ ppm vs year, a rising saw-toothed line. IN 0:54 (draws on left→right).
  STAYS to 1:00.
- **Callout** on the curve's end: "420 ppm — highest in millions of years." IN 0:58. OUT 1:00.
**VO:** "Every burned lump of coal, litre of petrol, cubic metre of gas releases carbon dioxide. We've
pushed it from about 280 parts per million before industry to over 420 today — higher than anything in
the human story."

---

## SCENE 5 — The Evidence: Locked Together (1:00 – 1:18)
**Goal:** CO₂ and temperature move together — and both are now spiking.
**Sees:** An **ice core** cylinder tilts into frame; a magnifier reveals tiny trapped air bubbles
("ancient air"). Then two overlaid curves stretch back **800,000 years**: a CO₂ line and a temperature
line rising and falling **in lockstep** through ice ages — and at the far right, both shoot **straight
up off the top** in a way the past never did.
- **Ice core + magnifier + bubbles** — left. IN 1:00 (slides in). OUT 1:08 (fade to the graph).
- **Dual curve chart** (CO₂ + temperature over 800k yr) — IN 1:08 (draws right-to-present). STAYS to 1:18.
- **"They move together" bracket** — links the two wiggling curves mid-graph. IN 1:12. OUT 1:16.
- **Red spike marker** on the modern end — a vertical flag "us, now." IN 1:15. STAYS to 1:18.
**VO:** "We can read the past in air bubbles frozen in ice. For eight hundred thousand years, carbon
dioxide and temperature rose and fell together — and today, both are spiking off the chart. This isn't
a natural cycle. It's us."

---

## SCENE 6 — A Feedback Loop: Melting Mirrors (1:18 – 1:36)
**Goal:** warming causes more warming — bright ice vs dark water (the albedo feedback).
**Sees:** A polar cap seen from above: **white ice** reflects incoming sunlight (arrows bounce back up).
As it warms, the ice **shrinks** and **dark ocean** spreads; now the arrows are **absorbed**, not
reflected — the water heats, melting more ice. A **circular arrow loop** labels the runaway: warm →
melt → darker → absorb more → warmer.
- **Ice cap (white)** — center, reflecting arrows bouncing up. IN 1:18. shrinks over 1:22–1:32. OUT 1:36.
- **Dark ocean** — spreads outward from the shrinking edge, absorbing arrows. IN 1:24 (grows). STAYS to 1:36.
- **Reflect arrows (up)** over ice / **Absorb arrows (in)** over water — IN with their surface. OUT 1:36.
- **Feedback loop ring** — a circular arrow with 4 labels (warm → melt → darker → absorb). IN 1:30.
  OUT 1:36.
**VO:** "And warming feeds itself. Bright ice reflects sunlight straight back to space. Melt it, and dark
ocean takes its place — soaking up heat, melting still more ice. A loop that, once pushed, keeps pushing
itself."

---

## SCENE 7 — What Warming Does (1:36 – 1:52)
**Goal:** the consequences — heat, seas, weather — shown, not listed.
**Sees:** A world map washes in and **flushes from blue to deep red** as a "global temperature anomaly"
scale fills. A coastline zoom shows the **sea line creeping up** over a low city edge. Small icons pulse
where extremes cluster — a flame (heatwave/fire), a storm swirl, a dry cracked patch — each briefly
labeled.
- **World map (heat)** — center, recolors blue→red. IN 1:36 (fades in, then recolors 1:38–1:46). OUT 1:52.
- **Temperature scale legend** — side bar, cool→hot. IN 1:38. OUT 1:52.
- **Coastline inset** — small panel, sea line rising over a city edge. IN 1:44 (rises). OUT 1:52.
- **Extreme-weather icons** (flame · storm · drought) — pulse on the map at 3 spots, each with a one-word
  label. IN 1:47 (stagger). OUT 1:52.
**VO:** "A degree or two doesn't sound like much — until you see it. Seas rise as ice melts and water
expands. Heatwaves, storms, and droughts grow fiercer and more frequent. The map of normal is being
redrawn."

---

## SCENE 8 — The Fork: It's the Amount (1:52 – 2:08)
**Goal:** the future isn't fixed — how much we emit sets how hot it gets.
**Sees:** One chart, two futures. A temperature line runs to today, then **splits into two branches**: a
**red branch** climbing steeply (high emissions) and a **green branch** bending back down toward flat
(rapid cuts). The gap between them widens dramatically toward 2100, shaded to show "the difference we
choose."
- **Temperature line to present** — IN 1:52 (draws). STAYS.
- **Red branch (high emissions)** — climbs steeply from the split. IN 1:56 (draws up). STAYS to 2:08.
- **Green branch (deep cuts)** — bends down to near-flat. IN 1:59 (draws). STAYS to 2:08.
- **Shaded gap + label** "This gap is a choice." — between the branches. IN 2:03. OUT 2:08.
**VO:** "Here's the hopeful part: nothing here is fixed. The temperature we land on depends almost
entirely on how much more carbon we add. Same planet, two very different futures — and the gap between
them is a choice."

---

## SCENE 9 — Turning the Curve (2:08 – 2:22)
**Goal:** the levers — swap fossil fuels for clean energy, and the emissions curve bends down.
**Sees:** On the left, **coal/oil/gas** icons fade out; on the right, **solar panels and wind turbines**
(blades spinning) fade in and grow. Below, an **emissions bar/curve bends from rising to falling** as
the swap happens; a small tree/forest strip regrows along the baseline.
- **Fossil icons (coal/oil/gas)** — left. IN 2:08. OUT 2:16 (fade out as they're replaced).
- **Clean-energy icons (solar + spinning turbines)** — right. IN 2:10 (grow in, blades spin). STAYS to 2:22.
- **Emissions curve** — bends from up to down across the scene. IN 2:12 (redraws downward). STAYS to 2:22.
- **Regrowing forest strip** — baseline. IN 2:17 (grows). STAYS to 2:22.
**VO:** "We already have the levers. Replace fossil fuels with sun, wind, and clean power. Use energy
wisely. Protect the forests that pull carbon back down. Do it fast enough, and the curve that's been
climbing for a century finally bends."

---

## SCENE 10 — The Whole Chain (2:22 – 2:34)
**Goal:** recap the causal chain in one clean diagram, ending on the lever.
**Sees:** Everything clears to a simple horizontal chain that **lights up left to right**: 🏭 **CO₂ up →
🌡️ heat trapped → 📈 temperature up → 🌊 impacts**. Then a single upstream arrow labeled **"cut
emissions"** loops back to the first box and dims the whole chain — showing where we intervene.
- **Chain of 4 boxes** — center, lights up left→right. IN 2:22 (stagger). STAYS to end.
- **"Cut emissions" lever arrow** — loops from the end back to CO₂, dimming the chain. IN 2:29. STAYS.
- **Takeaway** "We changed the air. We can change it back." — bottom. IN 2:31 (word-by-word). STAYS to 2:34.
**VO:** "So the whole story in one line: more carbon dioxide traps more heat, temperatures climb, and the
world feels it. We wrote that chain — which means we can rewrite it."
